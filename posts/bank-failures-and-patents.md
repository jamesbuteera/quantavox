Banks serve a monumental purpose — ranging from the custody of deposits to the strategic lending of those funds to third parties. They operate like a hidden motor, rarely seen or celebrated, yet their impact is felt in every facet of human endeavor.

When banks are successful, the economy flourishes. However, when a bank's doors close permanently, panic ensues.

## The research question

When banks fail, do patent filings increase or decrease? By analyzing these trends across local, small, and large-scale banks, I aim to determine if the success of American innovation is intrinsically tied to the health of our banking institutions.

## Silicon Valley Bank as a case study

The collapse of Silicon Valley Bank sent shockwaves not only through the financial sector but also through the world's most active entrepreneurial hubs. This event serves as a modern case study for my research.

## Methodology

My approach involves building a comprehensive Python-based dataset that links FDIC bank failure records with USPTO patent filing data across multiple time periods and geographic regions.

## What the data is telling us so far

Early analysis suggests that the relationship between bank failures and patent activity is not linear. This research is ongoing.
